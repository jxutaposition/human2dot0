About AgentBoard

We're building a marketplace where anyone can access, build, and own AI agents — no engineering degree required.

What is AgentBoard?

AgentBoard is a marketplace for task-specific AI agents. Instead of building custom AI solutions from scratch — something that requires engineering teams and six-figure budgets — anyone can browse a catalog of ready-to-deploy agents built by domain experts who understand the work.

But AgentBoard isn't just for buyers. Anyone with domain expertise can train and list agents on the platform — no engineering background required. A veteran grant writer can package their process into a Grant Writer agent. An accountant can build an Expense Tracker. The people closest to the work become the builders, and they own what they create.

Why We're Building This

There is an access gap in AI. Large enterprises can afford to build custom AI workflows. Small organizations, nonprofits, solo operators, and communities in the Global South cannot. They're left waiting for tools that may never reach them — or that arrive stripped of context.

We believe the people doing the work should have direct access to the tools that multiply it. AgentBoard exists to close that gap: not by building a single product for everyone, but by creating infrastructure where thousands of specialized agents can emerge from the people who know the work best.

The Research Behind It

AgentBoard emerged from research into BPO (Business Process Outsourcing) labor markets and how AI is reshaping knowledge work. The insight: most "AI disruption" flows through enterprises, leaving small organizations and independent workers behind.

Workers in outsourced roles — data entry, customer support, content moderation — are often the first displaced by AI, yet the last to benefit from it. AgentBoard is built on the premise that these same workers, retrained as agent builders, can own the AI tools that serve their industries. The marketplace model turns displacement into participation.
